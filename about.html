Our Story

More Than a Hospital —
A Health Community

2004 Founded

VitalCare was founded in 2004 by a small group of physicians who shared a simple but powerful belief: every Nigerian deserves world-class healthcare, regardless of their background or income level.

What started as a 20-bed clinic in Surulere, Lagos, has grown into a network of three full-service hospitals across Lagos and Abuja — serving over 12,000 patients annually with more than 48 specialist consultants.

We've pioneered minimally invasive robotic surgery in Nigeria, established Lagos's first dedicated private Stroke Unit, and launched free annual screening camps that have reached over 50,000 underserved Nigerians since 2010.

Today, VitalCare is JCI accredited, ISO 9001 certified, and consistently ranked among the top private hospital groups in West Africa.

Our Journey

20 Years of Milestones

2004

VitalCare Founded

Opened a 20-bed clinic in Surulere, Lagos. First week: 42 patients. First surgery: appendectomy performed by Dr. Adeyemi.

2008

Victoria Island Flagship Opens

Relocated to 14 Ozumba Mbadiwe Avenue with a 120-bed facility — marking our transformation into a full-service hospital.

2010

Free Screening Programme Launched

First annual community health screening camp reaches 3,000 underserved Lagosians. Programme now runs across 12 states.

2012

Lekki Hospital Opens

140-bed Lekki Phase 1 facility opened, including Nigeria's first private IVF Centre ranked in the top 3 nationally.

2015

First Private Stroke Unit in Lagos

Launched Lagos's first dedicated private Stroke Unit with 24/7 thrombolysis capability, cutting stroke mortality rates by 38%.

2017

JCI Accreditation Achieved

VitalCare Victoria Island becomes only the third Nigerian hospital to achieve Joint Commission International accreditation.

2018

Abuja Campus Opens

Expanded to the Federal Capital Territory with our 80-bed Wuse II facility, catering to Abuja's diplomatic and corporate community.

2023

Robotic Surgery Programme Launched

Installed Nigeria's first Da Vinci robotic surgery system in private healthcare, opening a new era of minimally invasive surgery.
